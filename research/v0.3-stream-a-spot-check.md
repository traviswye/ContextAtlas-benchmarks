# v0.3 Stream A Fix 2 spot-check — narrow_attribution on p4-stream-lifecycle

**Status:** Step 5 spot-check evidence for Step 7 decision.
Scratch format. Phase 6 §3 (metrics table) + §5.1 (mechanism)
+ [`atlas-claim-attribution-ranking.md`](atlas-claim-attribution-ranking.md)
are the upstream framing. Stream D (Step 15) is the rigorous
measurement; this note is informational input to Step 7's
ship decision for v0.3 default. Single-cell n=1 — see Caveats.

## Methodology

Two atlas variants were re-extracted from the httpx benchmark
fixture using the new `narrow_attribution` flag landed in
contextatlas Step 5 commit `7e1956a`:

- **Option A** (`--narrow-attribution=drop`): drop frontmatter
  inheritance entirely. Cleanest experimental knob; isolates
  the Phase 6 §5.1 mechanism check.
- **Option E** (`--narrow-attribution=drop-with-fallback`):
  drop, but fall back to frontmatter when a claim resolves to
  zero symbols. Cheap insurance against Option A's regression
  risk.

Each variant was used as the atlas substrate for a single p4
ca cell run (alpha is invariant to atlas changes per Phase
6 §5.1 framing; only ca exercises the atlas). Phase 6's
baseline ca p4 metrics (14 calls, 60.8k tokens — §3 line 102)
are the comparison anchor for trace-shape; this note does NOT
re-run alpha or other cells. Stream D (Step 15) re-runs the
full matrix.

### Reproduction

```powershell
# Option A — narrow-drop config
(Get-Content configs/httpx.yml -Raw) -replace 'atlases/httpx/', 'atlases/httpx-narrow-drop/' | Set-Content configs/httpx-narrow-drop.yml -Encoding utf8

# Option E — narrow-fallback config
(Get-Content configs/httpx.yml -Raw) -replace 'atlases/httpx/', 'atlases/httpx-narrow-fallback/' | Set-Content configs/httpx-narrow-fallback.yml -Encoding utf8

# Re-extract per option
node node_modules/contextatlas/dist/index.js index `
  --config-root="$($PWD.Path)" `
  --config=configs/httpx-narrow-drop.yml `
  --narrow-attribution=drop --json

node node_modules/contextatlas/dist/index.js index `
  --config-root="$($PWD.Path)" `
  --config=configs/httpx-narrow-fallback.yml `
  --narrow-attribution=drop-with-fallback --json

# Cell run (after temporarily redirecting configs/httpx.yml at the variant atlas;
# git checkout configs/httpx.yml restores after)
npx tsx scripts/run-reference.ts --repo=httpx --prompts=p4-stream-lifecycle `
  --conditions=ca --skip-preflight --ceiling=2.00
```

Atlases at `atlases/httpx-narrow-drop/` and
`atlases/httpx-narrow-fallback/` are gitignored; configs and
atlases reproduce mechanically from the commands above. Trace
files saved to `$env:TEMP` post-run.

## Atlas-level diff: zero-symbol claim recovery

Both variants extracted **75 claims** total (same prose
substrate). The flag affects per-claim symbol attachment:

| Variant | Total claims | Zero-symbol claims | …of which from ADR-05 (p4-relevant) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Option A (`drop`) | 75 | **17** | **8** |
| Option E (`drop-with-fallback`) | 75 | **0** | **0** |

**Reading.** Option A's regression risk materialized exactly
as the inventory note predicted: 17 of 75 claims (23%) lost
all symbol attachment, including 8 from ADR-05 specifically —
the ADR that answers the p4 prompt. Option E's fallback
recovered every one of them. The fallback mechanism works as
designed; this is the load-bearing atlas-level evidence that
Option E is not just safer in theory but in observed practice.

## Cell-level mechanism check: top-INTENT lines

Phase 6 §5.1 documented the load-bearing failure: CA's
`get_symbol_context` queries on `Response`, `ResponseNotRead`,
and `BoundSyncStream` returned bundles whose **top INTENT line
was off-target** — a claim about *request-side* streaming
(uploads) instead of *response-side* lifecycle. The spot-check
exposes the same cell to each variant atlas to test whether
attribution-narrowing shifts the top INTENT.

> **Methodological note on the table below.** Each variant's
> model issued *different* `get_symbol_context` queries based
> on its evolving context (e.g., Option E pivoted to
> `file_hint=httpx/_models.py` after an initial disambiguation
> error; Option A queried `read` and `ResponseNotRead`
> directly; Phase 6 baseline used queries that aren't
> replicable here without re-spawning the Phase 6 trace). The
> table compares **what each model received from CA**, not
> parallel queries against parallel atlases. `n/a` entries
> mean "this model didn't issue that query," not "data
> missing."

| Symbol query | Phase 6 baseline (BEFORE) | Option A (drop) | Option E (fallback) |
|---|---|---|---|
| `content` (no file_hint) | "Request-side streaming uses…" (off-target) | "Request-side streaming uses…" (still off-target) | not queried this way |
| `content` w/ `file_hint=response` | n/a | n/a | `ERR disambiguation_required` |
| `content` w/ `file_hint=httpx/_models.py` `depth=deep` | n/a | n/a | "Request-side streaming…" still surfaces; EXCERPT field exposes more rationale |
| `read` | n/a | bundle returned, no INTENT block (no claims attached) | n/a |
| `ResponseNotRead` | unknown | on-target claim about response read-state | n/a |

**Reading.** Neither variant fully closes the §5.1 mechanism.
The "Request-side streaming…" off-target claim still surfaces
as the top INTENT for the `content` symbol under both Option
A and Option E. This is honest evidence: **Fix 2 (extraction-
side narrowing) alone does NOT eliminate the muddy-bundle
mechanism on this cell**. The mechanism Phase 6 §5.1
attributed to Gap 1 (frontmatter-inheritance dilution) is
mitigated *but not eliminated* — a portion of the off-target
attribution survives even after frontmatter inheritance is
dropped, indicating that the residual signal comes from
claim-specific candidate resolution itself (Gaps 2 + 3 in the
inventory note's framing). Step 6's Fix 3 (BM25 query-relevance
ranking) is positioned to address Gaps 2 + 3 directly.

What Fix 2 *does* deliver: each variant changes how CA
*navigates* away from the off-target signal, with measurable
efficiency improvements documented in the next sub-table.

## Cell-level trace shape comparison

| Metric | Phase 6 baseline (BEFORE) | Option A (drop) | Option E (fallback) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Tool calls (ca total) | 14 | **9** (-36%) | **6** (-57%) |
| `get_symbol_context` calls | (Phase 6 trace not re-extracted) | 3 | 2 |
| `find_by_intent` calls | (Phase 6 trace not re-extracted) | 1 | 1 |
| Grep fallback rounds | 2 (per §5.1 narrative) | 3 | 1 |
| Final answer cites ADR-05 by name | NO ("There's an ADR on this") | yes — single quote | yes — explicit primary attribution |
| Answer-quality bucket | "win" (correct) | "win" (correct) | "win" (correct) |

Phase 6 baseline call count + tokens cited from §3 line 102;
"There's an ADR on this" framing cited from §5.1 lines
174-176; "+2 Grep rounds" cited from §5.1 lines 172-174.

**Reading.** Both variants reduced tool calls vs Phase 6
baseline; Option E reduced more (-57% vs -36%). Both produce
correct answers; the difference is in how the answer is
*structured*. Option A's answer reconstructs the lifecycle
through source-code inspection (line refs throughout, ADR-05
cited via a single quote). Option E's answer leads with
explicit attribution: *"The intent registry surfaces a hard
architectural constraint (ADR-05) attached to `Response`,
`BoundSyncStream`, `BoundAsyncStream`, and `ResponseNotRead`"*
— this is exactly the framing Phase 6 §5.1 noted was missing
in the baseline ("There's an ADR on this" without naming
ADR-05).

**Mechanism interpretation:** Option E's `drop-with-fallback`
preserves the ADR-symbol relationship at the atlas layer for
claims that would otherwise become orphaned. CA can then
surface the ADR-05 → Response/ResponseNotRead/BoundSyncStream
relationship structurally, rather than reconstructing it
from source. This is the difference between "atlas as
navigation aid" and "atlas as primary attribution surface."

p4 (§3 line 102) but not cell-level cost USD. Stream D will

| Option E cell | 25.5k | $0.4777 |

**Reading.** Option E's cell run is **22% cheaper than

flat across variants (~$1.24 each, within $0.04). Option E's
fallback branch fires only on zero-symbol claims at
extraction time, so the per-claim API-call profile during
extraction is near-identical between variants — the
extraction-side cost parity here is expected, not a
measurement coincidence.

## Decision input for Step 7

**Variant recommendation (scoped):** *if Step 7 chooses to
include Fix 2 in v0.3 default* — whether F2-only or in the
"Both" config alongside Fix 3 — *the variant should be
`drop-with-fallback`, not `drop`*. Fix 2 ship/no-ship
decision is Step 7's call after reading Step 6's evidence
and considering the full config matrix.

Reasoning for the variant recommendation:

- **Recovers the regression risk Option A introduces.**
  Atlas-level: 17 zero-symbol claims (8 from ADR-05) → 0.
  Concrete protection against the "claims become invisible
  to `get_symbol_context`" failure Option A's design
  acknowledged.
- **Better cell-level efficiency on the only cell measured.**
  Option E: 6 calls, 25.5k tokens, $0.4777. Option A: 9
  calls, 32.1k tokens, $0.6140.
- **Better attribution clarity in the answer.** Option E's
  answer leads with explicit ADR-05 attribution to the four
  governed symbols — the framing Phase 6 §5.1 noted was
  missing from baseline. Option A reconstructs from source
  with line refs; ADR-05 cited but not as primary structure.
- **Cheap insurance.** Option E's only cost vs Option A is
  the ~10 LOC fallback branch in `writeClaimsForFile`. The
  fallback fires only when needed (zero-symbol claims).

**Open Step 7 questions this note does NOT pre-empt:**

- Whether Fix 2 ships at all in v0.3 (depends on Step 6 Fix
  3 evidence + matrix evaluation).
- Whether Fix 3 alone closes more of the §5.1 mechanism
  than Fix 2 alone (this note shows Fix 2 doesn't fully
  close it).
- Whether Both (F2-on + F3-on) composes multiplicatively, or
  if the residual mechanism Fix 2 leaves is exactly what Fix
  3 covers (or vice versa). Step 7's optional 4th spot-check
  addresses this if Steps 5 + 6 evidence both net-positive.

The full Step 7 decision matrix:

| Config | F2 | F3 | Step 5 evidence | Step 6 evidence | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | off | off | Phase 6 §3 / §5.1 | (Step 6 baseline) | reference |
| F2-only | drop-with-fallback | off | this note | n/a | candidate |
| F3-only | off | on | n/a | (pending Step 6) | candidate |
| Both | drop-with-fallback | on | n/a | n/a | candidate (4th spot-check optional) |

## Caveats

1. **Single-cell evidence (n=1).** Only p4-stream-lifecycle
   measured. Stream D (Step 15) re-runs all httpx cells across
   all configurations; that's the rigorous measurement. This
   spot-check informs Step 7's *direction* but doesn't
   validate it across the prompt distribution. Other cells may
   behave differently — e.g., a cell where the model already
   had strong claim-specific candidates would see less benefit
   from Option E's fallback (most claims would not be
   zero-symbol under Option A, so fallback rarely fires).
2. **Phase 6 baseline cost not directly re-extracted.** Phase
   6 §3 reports tokens (60.8k) + call count (14) for ca p4
   but not cell-level cost USD. Cost-comparison rows above
   restrict to Option A vs Option E. Stream D will provide
   direct baseline measurements for the chosen v0.3 default.
3. **Mechanism not fully closed by Fix 2 alone.** The
   off-target Request-side claim still surfaces as top INTENT
   for `content` queries under both variants. Fix 2 mitigates
   the impact (lower tool-call count, better final-answer
   attribution clarity) without eliminating the trigger. Fix
   3's BM25 ranking is positioned to address the residual.
4. **Claim count parity is non-trivially load-bearing.** Both
   variants produced 75 claims — same count, different symbol
   attachments. This means Fix 2 doesn't lose claims; it
   redistributes their symbol pointers. The atlas-level
   accounting is honest because no extraction information was
   discarded.
