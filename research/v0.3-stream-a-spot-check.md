# v0.3 Stream A Fix 2 spot-check — narrow_attribution on p4-stream-lifecycle

**Status:** Step 5 spot-check evidence for Step 7 decision.
Scratch format. Phase 6 §3 (metrics table) + §5.1 (mechanism)
+ [`atlas-claim-attribution-ranking.md`](atlas-claim-attribution-ranking.md)
are the upstream framing. Stream D (Step 15) is the rigorous
measurement; this note is informational input to Step 7's
ship decision for v0.3 default. Single-cell n=1 — see Caveats.

## Methodology

Two atlas variants were re-extracted from the httpx benchmark
fixture using the new `narrow_attribution` flag landed in
contextatlas Step 5 commit `7e1956a`:

- **Option A** (`--narrow-attribution=drop`): drop frontmatter
  inheritance entirely. Cleanest experimental knob; isolates
  the Phase 6 §5.1 mechanism check.
- **Option E** (`--narrow-attribution=drop-with-fallback`):
  drop, but fall back to frontmatter when a claim resolves to
  zero symbols. Cheap insurance against Option A's regression
  risk.

Each variant was used as the atlas substrate for a single p4
ca cell run (alpha is invariant to atlas changes per Phase
6 §5.1 framing; only ca exercises the atlas). Phase 6's
baseline ca p4 metrics (14 calls, 60.8k tokens — §3 line 102)
are the comparison anchor for trace-shape; this note does NOT
re-run alpha or other cells. Stream D (Step 15) re-runs the
full matrix.

# Cell run (after temporarily redirecting configs/httpx.yml at the variant atlas;
# git checkout configs/httpx.yml restores after)

  --conditions=ca --skip-preflight --ceiling=2.00

Atlases at `atlases/httpx-narrow-drop/` and
`atlases/httpx-narrow-fallback/` are gitignored; configs and

## Atlas-level diff: zero-symbol claim recovery

Both variants extracted **75 claims** total (same prose
substrate). The flag affects per-claim symbol attachment:

| Variant | Total claims | Zero-symbol claims | …of which from ADR-05 (p4-relevant) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Option A (`drop`) | 75 | **17** | **8** |
| Option E (`drop-with-fallback`) | 75 | **0** | **0** |

**Reading.** Option A's regression risk materialized exactly
as the inventory note predicted: 17 of 75 claims (23%) lost
all symbol attachment, including 8 from ADR-05 specifically —
the ADR that answers the p4 prompt. Option E's fallback
recovered every one of them. The fallback mechanism works as
designed; this is the load-bearing atlas-level evidence that
Option E is not just safer in theory but in observed practice.

## Cell-level mechanism check: top-INTENT lines

Phase 6 §5.1 documented the load-bearing failure: CA's
`get_symbol_context` queries on `Response`, `ResponseNotRead`,
and `BoundSyncStream` returned bundles whose **top INTENT line
was off-target** — a claim about *request-side* streaming
(uploads) instead of *response-side* lifecycle. The spot-check
exposes the same cell to each variant atlas to test whether
attribution-narrowing shifts the top INTENT.

> **Methodological note on the table below.** Each variant's
> model issued *different* `get_symbol_context` queries based
> on its evolving context (e.g., Option E pivoted to
> `file_hint=httpx/_models.py` after an initial disambiguation
> error; Option A queried `read` and `ResponseNotRead`
> directly; Phase 6 baseline used queries that aren't
> replicable here without re-spawning the Phase 6 trace). The
> table compares **what each model received from CA**, not
> parallel queries against parallel atlases. `n/a` entries
> mean "this model didn't issue that query," not "data
> missing."

| Symbol query | Phase 6 baseline (BEFORE) | Option A (drop) | Option E (fallback) |
|---|---|---|---|
| `content` (no file_hint) | "Request-side streaming uses…" (off-target) | "Request-side streaming uses…" (still off-target) | not queried this way |
| `content` w/ `file_hint=response` | n/a | n/a | `ERR disambiguation_required` |
| `content` w/ `file_hint=httpx/_models.py` `depth=deep` | n/a | n/a | "Request-side streaming…" still surfaces; EXCERPT field exposes more rationale |
| `read` | n/a | bundle returned, no INTENT block (no claims attached) | n/a |
| `ResponseNotRead` | unknown | on-target claim about response read-state | n/a |

**Reading.** Neither variant fully closes the §5.1 mechanism.
The "Request-side streaming…" off-target claim still surfaces
as the top INTENT for the `content` symbol under both Option
A and Option E. This is honest evidence: **Fix 2 (extraction-
side narrowing) alone does NOT eliminate the muddy-bundle
mechanism on this cell**. The mechanism Phase 6 §5.1
attributed to Gap 1 (frontmatter-inheritance dilution) is
mitigated *but not eliminated* — a portion of the off-target
attribution survives even after frontmatter inheritance is
dropped, indicating that the residual signal comes from
claim-specific candidate resolution itself (Gaps 2 + 3 in the
inventory note's framing). Step 6's Fix 3 (BM25 query-relevance
ranking) is positioned to address Gaps 2 + 3 directly.

What Fix 2 *does* deliver: each variant changes how CA
*navigates* away from the off-target signal, with measurable
efficiency improvements documented in the next sub-table.

**Reading.** Both variants reduced tool calls vs Phase 6
baseline; Option E reduced more (-57% vs -36%). Both produce
correct answers; the difference is in how the answer is
*structured*. Option A's answer reconstructs the lifecycle
through source-code inspection (line refs throughout, ADR-05
cited via a single quote). Option E's answer leads with
explicit attribution: *"The intent registry surfaces a hard
architectural constraint (ADR-05) attached to `Response`,
`BoundSyncStream`, `BoundAsyncStream`, and `ResponseNotRead`"*
— this is exactly the framing Phase 6 §5.1 noted was missing
in the baseline ("There's an ADR on this" without naming
ADR-05).

**Mechanism interpretation:** Option E's `drop-with-fallback`
preserves the ADR-symbol relationship at the atlas layer for
claims that would otherwise become orphaned. CA can then
surface the ADR-05 → Response/ResponseNotRead/BoundSyncStream
relationship structurally, rather than reconstructing it
from source. This is the difference between "atlas as
navigation aid" and "atlas as primary attribution surface."

## Caveats

1. **Single-cell evidence (n=1).** Only p4-stream-lifecycle
   measured. Stream D (Step 15) re-runs all httpx cells across
   all configurations; that's the rigorous measurement. This
   spot-check informs Step 7's *direction* but doesn't
   validate it across the prompt distribution. Other cells may
   behave differently — e.g., a cell where the model already
   had strong claim-specific candidates would see less benefit
   from Option E's fallback (most claims would not be
   zero-symbol under Option A, so fallback rarely fires).
2. **Phase 6 baseline cost not directly re-extracted.** Phase
   6 §3 reports tokens (60.8k) + call count (14) for ca p4
   but not cell-level cost USD. Cost-comparison rows above
   restrict to Option A vs Option E. Stream D will provide
   direct baseline measurements for the chosen v0.3 default.
3. **Mechanism not fully closed by Fix 2 alone.** The
   off-target Request-side claim still surfaces as top INTENT
   for `content` queries under both variants. Fix 2 mitigates
   the impact (lower tool-call count, better final-answer
   attribution clarity) without eliminating the trigger. Fix
   3's BM25 ranking is positioned to address the residual.
4. **Claim count parity is non-trivially load-bearing.** Both
   variants produced 75 claims — same count, different symbol
   attachments. This means Fix 2 doesn't lose claims; it
   redistributes their symbol pointers. The atlas-level
   accounting is honest because no extraction information was
   discarded.

# Fix 3 BM25 spot-check (Step 6 append)

**Status:** Step 6 spot-check evidence appended to this note per
STEP-PLAN-V0.3 instruction "Findings recorded in same scratch note
as Step 5." Combined with Fix 2 evidence above, this section
covers the Theme 1.2 spot-check substrate Step 7 reads. Single-cell,
unit-level (Path 1) only — see Caveats.

## Methodology

Step 6 ships BM25-on-`get_symbol_context` (ADR-16, contextatlas
commit `44f043c`), gated behind `mcp.symbol_context_bm25`
config flag + caller-provided `query` parameter. Fix 3 is purely
query-time (no atlas re-extraction needed); the Step 6 spot-check
reuses the existing Fix 2 atlases as substrate.

**Path 1 (unit-level).** Local Node script
[`scripts/spot-check-step6-bm25.mjs`](../scripts/spot-check-step6-bm25.mjs)
imports each atlas into an in-memory SQLite, calls `buildBundle`
twice per symbol (once with `bm25Query`, once without), compares
the resulting top INTENT lines + full claim ordering. Tests four
symbols Phase 6 §5.1 documented as muddy-bundle cases: `Response`,
`ResponseNotRead`, `BoundSyncStream`, `BoundAsyncStream`.

Query string under test: `"response stream lifecycle read state"` —
constructed from p4-stream-lifecycle prompt keywords.

Substrate variants (both run):

- **Phase 6 baseline atlas** (`atlases/httpx/atlas.json`): Fix 2
  off, isolates BM25's effect from prior attribution work.
- **Step 5 Fix 2 fallback atlas** (`atlases/httpx-narrow-fallback/`):
  Fix 2 (`drop-with-fallback`) applied. Measures BM25 *additional*
  contribution on top of Fix 2.

**Cost: $0.** No API calls; FTS5 BM25 is local SQLite.

**Path 2 (cell-level) deferred to Stream D.** See "Implementation
vs activation gap" section below for why Step 6 spot-check is
unit-level only.

### Reproduction

```powershell
cd ContextAtlas-benchmarks
node scripts/spot-check-step6-bm25.mjs
```

(Requires contextatlas dist rebuilt with Step 6 changes:
`cd ../contextatlas && npm run build`. The benchmarks repo's
`node_modules/contextatlas` is a `file:..` link, so the rebuild

## Atlas-level effect: top INTENT shifts on all four target symbols

BM25 with the test query shifts top INTENT for **every** target
symbol on **both** atlas substrates. The pattern is more specific
than "v0.2 ranking was arbitrary": **7 of 8 probe combinations
show BM25 promoting a `severity=context` claim above multiple
`severity=hard` claims.** This is chain α (ADR-16 Decision §2,
"BM25 dominates, severity is tiebreaker") doing its designed work
on real data — not the Phase 6 §5.1 within-tied-severity scenario,
which is a related but distinct mechanism (see "Reframing the
Phase 6 §5.1 narrative" sub-section below).

### Phase 6 baseline atlas (no Fix 2)

All 4 target symbols inherit the same 17-claim set under Phase 6
baseline (Gap 1 collapse — frontmatter inheritance fanned out the
same claim set to all four), so v0.2 ordering and BM25 ordering
are identical across the four. The data point per symbol is
therefore one v0.2 winner + one BM25 winner, applied uniformly:

| Phase | Top INTENT | Severity | Claim id | BM25 score | Reordering type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| v0.2 BASELINE | "Auth schemes declaring requires_response_body force body materialization even inside client.stream(...)" | hard | 64 | −6.308 (5th-best) | — |
| BM25 (query="response stream lifecycle read state") | **"Response bodies track read-state as Unread, Read, or Closed."** | **context** | 72 | **−11.931 (best)** | cross-severity promotion |

The BM25 winner is `severity=context` (deprioritized below all
hard-severity claims under v0.2's severity-first chain) but
ranks far ahead of the v0.2 winner on query-token match
(−11.931 vs −6.308). 4 of 4 cross-severity promotions on this
atlas — driven by Gap 1's claim-set collapse, all four symbols
exhibit identical reordering behavior.

**What this evidence specifically shows.** BM25 promotes the
`"Response bodies track read-state…"` claim — `severity=context`,
which v0.2's severity-first ordering correctly places below all
hard-severity claims for these symbols — to position 1 because the
query tokens (`"response"`, `"read"`, `"state"`) match it densely.
This is chain α's design feature, not a Gap 2 fix: chain α
explicitly trades severity primacy for query relevance when a
query is provided.

### Step 5 Fix 2 fallback atlas

Per-symbol claim count: 10 attached to each (Fix 2 dropped 7 from
the Phase 6 baseline by narrowing attribution). Severity
distribution per symbol: 4-5 hard, 1 soft, 4-5 context. v0.2
winner under severity-first ordering: `severity=hard`
`"Non-streaming client methods…"` (id=63).

| Symbol | v0.2 BASELINE top INTENT (severity=hard) | BM25 top INTENT | BM25 winner severity | Reordering type |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `Response` | "Non-streaming client methods fully materialize the response body into response.content before returning" | **"Response tracks three explicit read-states: Unread, Read, and Closed."** | context | cross-severity promotion |
| `ResponseNotRead` | (same baseline) | **"client.stream(...) is the only supported way to stream; constructing a Response directly is off the supported path."** | hard | within-severity reordering |
| `BoundSyncStream` | (same baseline) | **"BoundSyncStream / BoundAsyncStream wrap the raw transport stream, propagate close, and record response.elapsed…"** | context | cross-severity promotion |
| `BoundAsyncStream` | (same baseline) | (same) | context | cross-severity promotion |

Three observations on the Step 5 Fix 2 atlas data:

1. **Per-symbol-relevant top INTENTs.** Where Phase 6 baseline
   produced the same top INTENT for all four (Gap 1 collapse),
   the Step 5 atlas + BM25 produces **per-symbol-relevant top
   INTENTs**. `BoundSyncStream` / `BoundAsyncStream` get the
   `BoundSyncStream`-specific claim. `Response` gets a Response-
   specific claim. `ResponseNotRead` gets a Response-construction
   constraint claim (`"client.stream(...) is the only supported
   way"`) — the closest match for `"response read state"` query
   tokens among the claims attached to that symbol. Fix 2 +
   Fix 3 compose well: Fix 2 narrowed attribution to per-symbol
   relevant subsets; Fix 3 ranks within those subsets by query.
2. **Cross-severity vs within-severity is symbol-dependent.**
   3 of 4 symbols show context-severity claims promoted above
   hard claims. 1 symbol (`ResponseNotRead`) shows within-hard
   reordering — the BM25 winner is `severity=hard`, displacing a
   different `severity=hard` claim that v0.2 ordered first via
   `claim_id` tiebreaker. Both patterns are chain α working as
   designed; the difference reflects which claims happen to
   dominate the query-token-match scoring per symbol.
3. **Fix 2's effect on cross-severity promotion is small.** Fix 2
   drops some claims from each symbol's set but doesn't
   fundamentally change the severity distribution — the
   context-severity claims that BM25 promotes were already
   present in the Phase 6 baseline, ranked low by v0.2's severity-
   first chain. Fix 3's cross-severity promotion is the
   load-bearing mechanism in both atlas variants.

### Reframing the Phase 6 §5.1 narrative

Phase 6 §5.1 documented Gap 2 as "deterministic non-query-aware
ranking" within tied-severity claim sets. The original narrative:
17 hard-severity claims tied on (severity, source) → claim_id
breaks the tie arbitrarily → top INTENT effectively random
relative to query.

This evidence shows a **different** (and arguably broader)
mechanism: BM25 with chain α reorders **across** severity tiers
when query relevance dominates. The 7-of-8 cross-severity
promotion result is not "Gap 2 fix" — it's "chain α design choice
producing its intended effect." Step 7 should evaluate Fix 3 on
*this* dimension, not on the within-severity-tie scenario.

Whether cross-severity promotion is the right behavior is exactly
the chain α vs chain β decision ADR-16 §Decision 2 made (chain α
chosen because the muddy-bundle problem IS that severity-first
surfaces an off-target claim). The evidence here confirms chain α
fires aggressively in practice — context-severity claims will
displace hard claims for any query that matches them densely.

### Full ordering changes

For all 4 symbols × both atlases (8 combinations), BM25 produces
a *different* full ordering vs v0.2 — not just different top
INTENT. Sample (Step 5 atlas, `Response` symbol):

```
BASELINE (v0.2 ordering):
  1. Non-streaming client methods fully materialize the response body...
  2. Streaming responses must be obtained via Client.stream(...)
  3. client.stream(...) is the only supported way to stream...
  ...
  10. Response tracks three explicit read-states: Unread, Read, and Closed.

BM25 (query="response stream lifecycle read state"):
  1. Response tracks three explicit read-states: Unread, Read, and Closed.
  2. client.stream(...) is the only supported way to stream...
  3. response.read() is idempotent...
  ...
  8. Non-streaming client methods fully materialize the response body...
```

The `"Response tracks three explicit read-states…"` claim was at
position **10** under v0.2 (last) and moves to position **1** under
BM25. Reciprocally, the v0.2 winner moves to position 8.

## Implementation vs activation gap

**The BM25 ranking primitive works. CA, today, doesn't use it.**
Step 7 must consider this gap when evaluating Fix 3's
ship/no-ship — flagged here per Step 6 survey methodology
discussion.

### Five facts (each independently verified)

1. **Fix 3's mechanism works.** Path 1 unit-level evidence above
   demonstrates that BM25-with-query produces measurably different
   (and clearly more on-topic for the test query) top INTENTs vs
   v0.2 ordering on both Fix-2-on and Fix-2-off atlas substrates.
2. **Current ca-agent doesn't pass `query` parameters.** Phase 6
   ca traces show `get_symbol_context` calls of shape `{ symbol:
   "content" }` — no query string. The benchmarks repo's ca-agent
   in [`src/harness/ca-agent.ts`](../src/harness/ca-agent.ts)
   adapts the symbol-context tool from the MCP `tools/list`
   response and passes the model whatever schema it advertises;
   the model decides whether to use the new optional `query`
   parameter. Without prompt engineering or system-prompt
   guidance directing it to do so, the model has no incentive to.
3. **Activation requires work outside Fix 3's implementation.**
   Three plausible activation surfaces, each requiring evidence
   + design separately from Fix 3:
   - **Tool description engineering.** Update the
     `get_symbol_context` tool description in
     [`src/mcp/schemas.ts`](../../contextatlas/src/mcp/schemas.ts)
     to instruct the model to pass a `query` when applicable.
     Cheapest path; affects every CA-style client universally
     since they all read `tools/list`.
   - **System prompt updates.** Benchmarks-side ca-agent system
     prompt could instruct the model to derive a query from the
     user prompt context. Per-harness rather than universal.
   - **Caller-side query synthesis.** ca-agent (or the wrapping
     framework) could compute a query from the user prompt and
     inject it into every `get_symbol_context` call. Most
     invasive; loses the "model decides what's relevant" framing.
4. **Without activation, F3-on at the contextatlas server side =
   dead code on the client side.** The
   `mcp.symbol_context_bm25=true` config setting alone, without a
   client passing queries, is byte-equivalent to
   `mcp.symbol_context_bm25=false` (per the two-layer gating
   contract: flag ON + query ABSENT → v0.2 fallback). Free to
   enable, but provides zero observable benefit on cells where
   the client doesn't send queries.
5. **Stream D measurement of F3 is conditional on activation
   work happening between Step 7 ship and Stream D execution.**
   If the activation surfaces above are not exercised in the
   Stream D harness setup, Stream D's F3-on measurements would
   reflect dead-code overhead (effectively re-measuring v0.2
   baseline plus harmless code paths). Step 7's decision should
   either (a) include activation work in v0.3 scope as a
   precondition to shipping F3-on, or (b) ship F3 as flag-
   accessible-but-default-off and treat activation as
   evidence-gated post-v0.3 work.

### Caveat (Step 7 dialogue insight): production-vs-benchmark distinction

The Five Facts above are accurate for the synthetic ca-agent
harness — the measurement substrate this evidence note runs on.
Step 7 dialogue surfaced a distinction they under-state: the
synthetic ca-agent is *not* the production target. The production
target is Claude Code with CA MCP (harness condition `beta-ca`);
`alpha` / `ca` / `beta` are measurement substrates, not the
user-facing product surface.

The same distinction now lives in ADR-16 (amendment block,
contextatlas commit `0bf5e2d`) and ROADMAP.md (refresh commit
`f33113b`, "What ContextAtlas Is FOR" — canonical product-
positioning reference). Two-line summary:

- **Synthetic ca-agent activation gap** is empirically verified
  here (Phase 6 trace shape `{ symbol: "content" }`, fact #2).
- **Claude Code production activation** under an updated tool
  description is *empirically unverified* — qualitatively
  different from the synthetic harness (different model, system
  prompt, tool-selection priors); the production gap may be
  smaller, larger, or absent.

**Implication for evidence-note interpretation.** Step 6's load-
bearing Path-1 finding — that the BM25 mechanism works — stands
unchanged. The caveat clarifies that *production* effectiveness
of Fix 3 is empirically open; Step 6 demonstrates the mechanism,
not production activation.

**Non-goals.** Doesn't change the Step 6 spot-check methodology,
doesn't reopen Step 7's locked decision framework, doesn't
invalidate the Five Facts under their original synthetic-harness
framing — anchors that framing as one substrate among several.

| Baseline (off/off) | not shipped | not shipped | no |
| F2-only (`drop-with-fallback`) | active at extraction | not enabled | **none — F2 is purely server-side, Step 5 evidence stands** |
| F3-only | not shipped | enabled | **yes — see facts 3-5; without activation, equivalent to baseline** |
| Both | active at extraction | enabled | yes (for F3 portion only) |

**Step 7's decision should treat activation as scope.** Options:

- **Ship F3-on as v0.3 default** AND include at least
  tool-description engineering in Step 7's scope (cheapest
  activation). Stream D measures end-to-end.
- **Ship F3-flag-accessible-but-default-off.** No activation
  work in v0.3; F3 stays available for future evidence-gated
  activation. Stream D measures it as no-op vs baseline; users
  who flip the flag without activation see no benefit (and no
  regression).
- **Defer F3 entirely** (rescope per `v0.3-SCOPE.md` rescope
  condition #1). Implementation lands as flag-accessible code
  in the v0.3 ship; activation work plus measurement deferred
  to v0.4. Honest if the activation engineering is judged
  out-of-scope for v0.3.

The variant choice (F3 ship as default-off opt-in vs flag-
accessible-only) carries the same Stream-D-measures-it
observability as the F2 case — both options keep the
implementation in the codebase for future evidence-gated
activation.

## Decision input for Step 7

**Implementation status (scoped):** *if Step 7 chooses to
include Fix 3 in v0.3* — whether F3-only or in the "Both" config
alongside Fix 2 — *the implementation here is sound and
correct on the dimensions tested*. The two ship-blocker canaries
in
[`src/queries/symbol-context.test.ts`](../../contextatlas/src/queries/symbol-context.test.ts)
guarantee v0.2-equivalence when query is absent (parallel role
to Step 4's `BYTE_EQUIVALENCE_EXPECTED` and Step 5's
v0.2-equivalence canary; 3-data-point canary discipline).

**Two design questions Step 7 must answer**, beyond the
ship/no-ship binary:

### Design question 1 — chain α aggressive cross-severity behavior

7 of 8 probe combinations show BM25 promoting context-severity
claims above hard-severity claims (per the Atlas-level effect
section above). This is chain α (ADR-16 Decision §2) producing
its intended effect — but "intended" doesn't mean "agreed by
Step 7 as the right v0.3 default." Two interpretations:

- **Pro chain α:** when caller passes a query, they're explicitly
  opting in to query-relevance ranking. Severity-first ordering
  is what they get without a query. The two-layer gating ensures
  cross-severity promotion only happens on opt-in calls.
- **Con chain α:** severity is a load-bearing signal in the v0.2
  contract — `hard` claims are "must do this," `context` claims
  are "background." Promoting a context claim above hard claims
  for query-relevance reasons could mislead callers who assume
  top INTENT carries severity-first semantics regardless of how
  it was ordered.

**ADR-16 already chose chain α**, citing the muddy-bundle problem
as motivation. The evidence here doesn't reopen that choice — it
confirms chain α's behavior empirically. Step 7 should consider
whether documentation (DESIGN.md tool description, ADR-16's
caveats) sufficiently surfaces "BM25 may promote
lower-severity-but-more-relevant claims" so callers calibrate
expectations.

### Design question 2 — activation matters more than implementation

Per the implementation-vs-activation gap section above. Even with
the implementation correct and chain α behavior empirically
verified, F3-on without client-side query-passing is dead code.
Step 7's v0.3-default decision turns on activation, not
implementation:

- **F3 ships at all** depends on activation engineering being
  in-scope for v0.3 (probably tool-description engineering at
  minimum, ~5-10 LOC).
- **F3 ships default-on or flag-accessible-only** is Step 7's
  call after considering Stream D's measurement plan.
- **The variant ranking (drop-with-fallback for F2)** locked in
  Step 5's evidence is independent of Step 6's findings; F3
  composes additively or multiplicatively on top of any F2
  variant choice.

## Caveats

1. **Single query string measured.** "response stream lifecycle
   read state" is one of many plausible queries the model might
   synthesize for the p4 prompt. Different queries would produce
   different ranking outcomes. Stream D's full-matrix
   measurement (after activation engineering lands) is the
   authoritative test of "does BM25 help under realistic CA
   query distributions."
2. **Single-cell evidence (n=1).** Only p4-stream-lifecycle
   symbols probed. Stream D (Step 15) measures across all p1-p6
   on httpx + hono + cobra.
3. **Path 1 unit-level only.** Cell-level (Path 2) measurement
   would require harness modification to inject queries into
   ca-agent calls. Deferred per the implementation-vs-activation
   gap framing — Stream D is the rigorous measurement vehicle.
4. **No activation engineering in Step 6 scope.** This step
   ships the BM25 ranking primitive; activation engineering
   (tool description updates, system prompt guidance, etc.) is
   Step 7's scope decision per the matrix above.
5. **BM25 score is not calibrated across queries.** Per ADR-09's
   discipline (no `relevance` score in tool responses): the
   numeric scores above (e.g., −11.931) are diagnostic, not part
   of the wire format. Step 7 should not over-trust score
   magnitudes; the directional finding ("BM25 ranks more
   on-topic claims first") is the load-bearing claim.
6. **POSITIVE_INFINITY sentinel preserved.** All claims attached
   to the symbol still surface (BM25 only re-orders; never
   filters). Verified via "Total count parity: OK" in every
   probe — pre-fix and post-fix bundle sizes are identical.
